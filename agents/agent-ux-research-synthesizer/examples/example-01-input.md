# Input: UX research notes

**Method:** Contextual inquiry (6 sessions, 45 min each)
**Product context:** Mobile checkout redesign (see problem frame)

## Raw notes

**P1 (F, 32, frequent shopper):** "I just tap Apple Pay everywhere else. Here I have to type my card every time. I gave up twice last month."

**P2 (M, 28, occasional shopper):** "The progress bar shows 5 steps. I see that and I already feel tired. I usually do it on desktop instead."

**P3 (F, 37, frequent shopper):** "The error message just says 'payment failed'. I don't know if it's my card, the app, or my bank. I tried 3 times."

**P4 (M, 41, frequent shopper):** "I wish I could just confirm with Face ID. My card is already saved. Why do I need to see all these screens?"

**P5 (F, 29, occasional shopper):** "I don't trust the checkout. The design looks old. I check if there's a lock icon before I enter anything."

**P6 (M, 34, frequent shopper):** "The back button in the address step deleted everything I typed. I had to start over."
