## Group 1 — Design Systems: Theory and Methodology

### Atomic Design — Brad Frost
- Best for: understanding component-based architecture (atoms → molecules → organisms → templates → pages), modularity rationale, Pattern Lab, style guide maintenance.
- Key insight: the page metaphor is obsolete; design systems, not pages, are the right unit of work.
- Roles/agents: DS architects, component library builders, anyone creating or auditing Figma component structures.

### Design Systems — Alla Kholmatova (Smashing Magazine)
- Best for: understanding what makes a DS coherent — functional patterns (modules/behaviours) vs. perceptual patterns (brand/aesthetics), design principles, shared language, pattern libraries.
- Key insight: a DS is only as strong as the shared language and culture around it; tooling is secondary.
- Roles/agents: DS managers, DS QA agents, anyone writing or auditing design principles.

### Expressive Design Systems — Yesenia Perez-Cruz (A Book Apart)
- Best for: connecting DS to brand voice and visual identity; purpose statements; making room for variation without fragmentation; governance and contribution models.
- Key insight: expressive systems are purpose-built, support a range of expression, and inspire use — they are not rigid constraint machines.
- Roles/agents: brand-aligned design, DS expressiveness reviews, DS strategy agents.

## Group 2 — Design Systems: Practice, Governance, and Org

### Hackez le Design System — Idean France (FR)
- Best for: organisational change management around DS adoption; governance models; KPI definition; cross-team collaboration; real-world success stories (Adobe, SNCF, Crédit Agricole, BPCE).
- Key insight: strict vs. loose systems, team engagement, accessibility as a DS feature, communication as a first-class DS responsibility.
- Roles/agents: DS governance agents, org-level strategy, stakeholder communication. French-language reference, valuable for French-speaking teams.

### Design Systems Handbook — InVision / DesignBetter.co
- Best for: end-to-end DS creation workflow (introducing → designing → building → implementing → scaling); team roles; reducing design debt; cross-functional collaboration.
- Key insight: a DS unites product teams around a common visual language; it reduces design debt and builds bridges between disciplines.
- Roles/agents: DS managers, product designers adopting a DS for the first time, onboarding workflows.

## Group 3 — UX/UI Broad Reference

### UI/UX Design Guide — Joel Parker Henderson (EPUB)
- Best for: definitions and best practices across the full UX/UI stack — IA, task analysis, personas, journeys, accessibility (WCAG, ARIA, screen readers, colour blindness), ideation, usability heuristics, modelling diagrams, copywriting, localization, testing, AI in UX.
- Key insight: encyclopaedic reference, not a narrative. Best used as a lookup source for definitions, method descriptions, and checklists.
- Roles/agents: accessibility agent, UX research agent, UX writing / content agent, any agent needing a precise definition of a UX concept.
